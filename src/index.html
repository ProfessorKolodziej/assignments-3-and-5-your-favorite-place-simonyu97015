Ginzan Onsen

Yamagata Prefecture | Japan

Ginzan Onsen is a hot spring area in Obanazawa, Yamagata Prefecture, Japan. Its name means "silver mine hot spring". Ginzan Onsen's economy grew due to silver mining and production and eventually transitioned to tourism when it opened dozens of hot spring resorts and ryokan along the central river that runs through the town. The mountains that surround this town yield rich hot spring water that is used both in the private hotels and public baths in the city center.

Must Visit Places

  1. Ginzan Onsen Historic District

    The Ginzan Onsen Historic District is renowned for its picturesque hot spring village lined with historic ryokan (Japanese inns) and wooden buildings. Visitors can stroll along the lantern-lit streets and enjoy the ambiance of a bygone era.

    See Detail

  2. Shirogane Falls

    Shirogane Falls, also known as the "White Silver Falls," is a breathtaking natural wonder located near Ginzan Onsen. The pristine cascade of water plunges into a tranquil pool, offering a serene and rejuvenating experience.

    See Detail

  3. Kogetsu Shuzo Sake Brewery

    Experience the art of sake brewing at Kogetsu Shuzo Sake Brewery. Learn about the traditional methods of sake production and indulge in tastings of premium sake varieties, crafted with pure mountain spring water.

    See Detail

  4. Obanazawa Mine Okama Crater

    Explore the dramatic landscape of the Obanazawa Mine Okama Crater, formed by volcanic activity centuries ago. The crater's striking greenish-blue waters and surrounding mountains offer a surreal and unforgettable sight.

    See Detail

  5. Ginzan Onsen Public Baths

    Immerse yourself in the healing waters of the Ginzan Onsen Public Baths. These traditional hot spring baths offer relaxation and rejuvenation, with picturesque views of the surrounding mountains.

Tips Before Traveling

  • Remember to bring cash, as some establishments may not accept credit cards.
  • Respect local customs, such as removing your shoes before entering a ryokan.
  • Try traditional Japanese cuisine, such as sushi and tempura, at local restaurants.
  • Learn a few basic Japanese phrases to enhance your travel experience.
  • Pack appropriate attire for each season, as temperatures can vary significantly.
  • Take advantage of foot baths located throughout the town for a relaxing soak.
  • Check the weather forecast before planning outdoor activities to ensure an enjoyable experience.

Local Cuisine

Ginzan Onsen is renowned for its delicious local cuisine, featuring fresh mountain ingredients and traditional Japanese dishes. Don't miss the opportunity to savor specialties like "onsen tamago" (hot spring eggs), "sasakamaboko" (bamboo leaf-shaped fish cake), and "yamagata soba" (buckwheat noodles). Indulge in the flavors of the region and experience the culinary delights of Ginzan Onsen.
